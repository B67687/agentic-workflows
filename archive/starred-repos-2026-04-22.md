2. **Token efficiency is a theme** — caveman (65% reduction), pretext (accurate text measurement), get-shit-done (context engineering). Not just starring LLMs but tools that make them more efficient.

3. **Learning/education is a second major thread** — ~35 repos are programming education, CS curricula, or project-based learning. Connected to the user's own learning journey (CS50P, H2 Computing, MathLearningNotes).

4. **Strong Windows/Android utility interest** — Windows tooling (PowerToys, winutil, Flow.Launcher, GlazeWM) and Android modding (ReVanced, xManager, LibreTube) show platform-level customization interest.

5. **China-related content is significant** — 8 repos in Chinese, including two "weekly" newsletters (ruanyf, ascoders) and Chinese poetry. Indicates regular engagement with Chinese-language tech content.

6. **Own projects are minimally starred** — Only 5 own repos starred, mostly with 1 star each. Own work (MathLearningNotes, CS50p-2022, H2-Computing) is the content, not the stars.

7. **No explicit AI detection / voice cloning tools** — Despite having done extensive AI detection evasion research, no starred repos in that space. Indicates research is applied via prompting, not tooling.
