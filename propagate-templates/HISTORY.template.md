## Why Keep History?

- **Resume faster:** After a break, read the last 2–3 entries instead of re-exploring the whole repo.
- **Capture external work:** Not everything lives in git. Research, conversations, experiments elsewhere — log them here so they don't get lost.
- **Avoid repeating mistakes:** Note what didn't work alongside what did.
- **Handover ready:** If another agent or human takes over, they get context in 30 seconds.
- **Decision trail:** Know why things are the way they are. "Why did we choose X?" → check history.

## What Counts as "Significant"

Add an entry when you:
- Create or delete files that matter
- Change architecture or conventions
- Fix something that took real debugging
- Make a decision that future work depends on
- Complete a multi-step task
- Do related work outside this repo (research, prototyping, discussions)

Skip it when you:
- Fix a one-line typo
- Run a routine command with no surprises
- Make a change that is 100% self-evident from git diff

## Style Tips

- **One entry per session** (or per major phase if the session is huge).
- **Bullet lists > paragraphs.** Faster to scan.
- **Link to deep docs** instead of pasting them. Keep the entry compact.
- **Include verification.** "It works because I ran X and saw Y."
- **Date everything.** Use `YYYY-MM-DD` format.
- **Log external work explicitly.** Even a one-liner with a URL saves future searching.

## High-Activity Projects

Keep this root `HISTORY.md` as a short index pointing to the monthly files, or retire it in favor of the `history/` folder.
